Theoretical Framework for Sustainable Conservation

  1. Ecological Sustainability. Utilize eco-friendly materials, renewable energy, and adaptive reuse to reduce environmental impact while preserving heritage authenticity.
  2. Economic Viability.  Leverage heritage tourism revenue, offer financial incentives, and promote heritage-related businesses to ensure long-term funding for conservation.
  3. Preventive Maintenance. Implement routine inspections and proactive measures to address wear and tear, reducing costly restorations and preserving structural integrity.
  4. Integration in Urban Planning. Incorporate heritage assets into urban master plans, balancing modernization with preservation to enhance both cultural and urban resilience
  5. Community Involvement. Empower local communities to participate in conservation efforts, fostering ownership, pride, and the preservation of traditional knowledge.

Prof. Badaruddin Mohamed, Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM)

"Intangible cultural heritage and tangible assets successfully create a sense of place and ensure the sustainable development of places."

Prof. Ahmad Bashawir Abdul Ghani, Universiti Utara Malaysia (UUM)
