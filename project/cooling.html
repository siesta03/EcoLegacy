How Trees help cool the city

How trees effect the cooling process

Explanation
further explanation of cooling process that effect the city.
  1. Shading. Tree canopies block sunlight from hitting streets, sidewalks, buildings, and other urban surfaces, which can absorb and radiate significant heat.
  2.  Evapotranspiration. Moisture from soil and plant surfaces evaporates into the air that will absorbs heat from the environment, creating a cooling effect.
  3. Carbon Sequestration. Trees absorb CO₂ during photosynthesis, reducing the concentration of this greenhouse gas in urban areas.
  4. Reduction of Heat Absorption. Trees reduce the amount of sunlight for the building surfaces to absorb by shading them, preventing the accumulation of heat.
  5. Airflow. Trees can influence wind patterns, make a way for airflow that disperses heat.
